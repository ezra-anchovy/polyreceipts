üéØ Brier Score

Measures prediction accuracy. 0 = perfect, 0.25 = random guessing, 1 = always wrong. Lower is better. A skilled trader should score below 0.2.

üìä Calibration Score

Do they know what they don't know? If someone bets at 70% implied odds, they should win ~70% of those. 100% = perfectly calibrated. Overconfident traders score low.

üí∞ Win Rate vs ROI

Win rate alone is misleading ‚Äî a 90% win rate on -900 odds is expected. ROI (return on investment) accounts for odds and is the true measure of profitability.

üé≤ Skill vs Luck (p-value)

"Trades to p<0.05" shows how many more bets needed to statistically prove skill. Fewer = stronger evidence. Many "whales" need 100+ more trades to prove they're not just lucky.
